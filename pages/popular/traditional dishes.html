A Taste of Tradition: Sri Lanka’s Culinary Heritage

Rooted in culture and tradition, Sri Lankan cuisine offers a unique blend of flavors, textures, and aromas that have been passed down through generations. From wholesome, hearty meals to delicately spiced delights, each dish reflects the island’s rich heritage.

RECIPE

Pittu

Sri Lankan Pittu – A beloved traditional dish made from a mixture of rice flour and freshly grated coconut, steamed to perfection in a cylindrical mold.loved for its unique texture and deliciously comforting taste.

RECIPE

Roti

Sri Lankan Roti – A traditional flatbread made with a blend of flour, coconut.Roti is best enjoyed warm with lunu miris. This hearty dish is a staple for breakfast or dinner and pairs beautifully with a hot cup of Ceylon tea.

RECIPE

Malu Abul Thiyal

Sri Lankan Malu Abul Thiyal – A unique and flavorful Sri Lankan fish curry that is known for its rich, tangy taste and ability to stay fresh for days without refrigeration. Malu Abul Thiyal is a must-try for those who love bold and authentic Sri Lankan flavors.

RECIPE

Kiribath (Milk Rice)

Kiribath – A sacred and symbolic dish in Sri Lankan culture, Kiribath (Milk Rice) is made by cooking rice in coconut milk until it reaches a rich, creamy texture. More than just a meal, Kiribath is an essential dish served during celebrations, and other auspicious occasions .

RECIPE

Hoppers

Hoppers – A crispy, bowl-shaped Sri Lankan delicacy made from a fermented rice flour and coconut milk batter. With its delightfully crisp edges and soft, spongy center. This dish remains a beloved staple in Sri Lankan cuisine.

RECIPE

String Hoppers (Idiyappam)

String Hoppers (Idiyappam) is a traditional Sri Lankan dish made from rice flour. The dough is made by mixing rice flour with hot water, then pressing it into fine strings using a special tool called an "Idiyappam maker."
